# Conversion Copywriting

Apply Eugene Schwartz's market awareness framework and AIDA structure to create copy that meets prospects where they are.

## When to Use

- Analyzing audience awareness before writing
- Writing headlines for cold vs warm traffic
- Structuring sales pages based on prospect journey
- Adapting copy for different traffic sources
- Determining how much education vs selling is needed

## The 5 Stages of Market Awareness

The prospect's awareness level determines everything: headline approach, copy length, proof needed, and overall strategy.

### Stage 1: Unaware
**Doesn't know they have a problem.**

- Lead with aspirational content, lifestyle, curiosity
- Long educational journey required
- Headlines: "The Hidden Reason Most Professionals Feel Stuck"
- Copy length: 5000+ words
- Path: Lead magnet → Email nurture → Deeper content → Sales

### Stage 2: Problem Aware
**Knows they have a problem but not that solutions exist.**

- Name and agitate the problem
- Position it as solvable
- Headlines: "Tired of Leads Going Cold Before You Can Close Them?"
- Copy length: 3000–5000 words
- Path: Problem-focused lead magnet → Education → Solution intro → Sales

### Stage 3: Solution Aware
**Knows solutions exist but not about YOUR solution.**

- Promise specific results, differentiate from alternatives
- Headlines: "A New Approach to Lead Generation That Actually Works"
- Copy length: 1500–3000 words
- Path: Direct to sales page or free trial/demo

### Stage 4: Product Aware
**Knows your product but hasn't purchased.**

- Testimonials and case studies are critical
- Address specific objections directly
- Headlines: "See Why 4,327 Business Owners Trust [Product]"
- Copy length: 800–1500 words
- Key: Remove remaining friction

### Stage 5: Most Aware
**Knows and wants your product, just needs a push.**

- Lead with offer and urgency
- Short, direct copy
- Headlines: "Last Chance: 40% Off Ends at Midnight"
- Copy length: 300–800 words
- Key: Make the decision easy

## Quick Reference: Awareness → Copy Approach

| Level | Primary Hook | Copy Focus | Length |
|-------|-------------|------------|--------|
| Unaware | Curiosity, lifestyle | Story → Problem reveal | 5000+ |
| Problem Aware | Pain, consequences | Validation → Solution exists | 3000–5000 |
| Solution Aware | Mechanism, differentiation | Why this works | 1500–3000 |
| Product Aware | Proof, objection handling | Confidence building | 800–1500 |
| Most Aware | Offer, urgency | Take action now | 300–800 |

## Traffic Temperature Mapping

| Traffic Source | Typical Awareness | Copy Approach |
|----------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Cold Facebook/Social Ads | Unaware / Problem Aware | Educational, curiosity-driven |
| Google Search (problem keywords) | Problem / Solution Aware | Direct, benefit-focused |
| Retargeting | Product Aware | Proof-heavy, objection handling |
| Email list (engaged) | Most Aware | Offer-focused, urgency |
| Referrals | Product Aware | Social proof, testimonials |

## The AIDA Framework

### Attention
Grab them in 3 seconds. Headlines, hooks, pattern interrupts.
- Use specific numbers
- Ask provocative questions
- Make bold claims
- Name their pain

### Interest
Keep them reading. Build the story.
- Share their situation back to them
- Introduce curiosity gaps
- Promise what's coming
- Make it relevant to THEM

### Desire
Make them want it.
- Paint the "after" picture
- Stack benefits (not features)
- Use social proof strategically
- Address objections proactively

### Action
Tell them exactly what to do.
- Clear, single CTA
- Remove friction
- Add urgency if genuine
- Reassure (guarantee, support)

## Market Sophistication Levels

How jaded is the market to marketing claims?

| Level | Strategy | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1: New Market | Direct claims work | "Lose weight fast" |
| 2: Second Stage | Enlarge the claim | "Lose 30 pounds in 30 days" |
| 3: Mechanism | Introduce HOW | "The Keto Method" |
| 4: Enhanced Mechanism | Better mechanism | "The Advanced Keto Protocol" |
| 5: Identification | Connect to identity | "For Busy Executives Who..." |

## Headline Writing by Awareness Level

| Awareness | Lead With | Avoid |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Unaware | Curiosity, lifestyle, aspiration | Product, solution, problem |
| Problem Aware | Pain, frustration, consequences | Product features, pricing |
| Solution Aware | Unique mechanism, differentiation | Generic benefits |
| Product Aware | Proof, specifics, objection handling | Same-old messaging |
| Most Aware | Offer, urgency, deal | Education, lengthy story |

## Headline Strategies by Stage

### Unaware Headlines
**Curiosity-Based:**
- "The Strange Habit That's Quietly Sabotaging Your Success"
- "What Happens When You Stop Doing This One Thing"

**Story-Based:**
- "How a Failed Salesman Became a Lead Generation Machine"
- "The Accidental Discovery That Changed Everything"

### Problem Aware Headlines
**Pain Articulation:**
- "Tired of Watching Leads Go Cold Before You Close Them?"
- "The Real Reason Your Marketing Feels Like Throwing Money Away"

**Validation-Based:**
- "If You've Ever Felt [Pain], You're Not Alone"
- "It's Not Your Fault That [Problem Exists]"

### Solution Aware Headlines
**Mechanism-Focused:**
- "The [Unique Name] System for [Desired Result]"
- "Why [Common Approach] Fails (And What Works Instead)"

**Differentiation:**
- "Unlike [Common Solution], This Actually [Benefit]"

### Product Aware Headlines
**Proof-Heavy:**
- "See the Exact Results Our Clients Are Getting"
- "Why 4,327 Business Owners Chose [Product Name]"

**Objection-Handling:**
- "Still Wondering If [Product] Is Right for You?"
- "What to Expect in Your First 30 Days"

### Most Aware Headlines
**Offer-Focused:**
- "Last Chance: 40% Off Ends Tonight"
- "Everything You Get When You Start Today"

**Urgency-Driven:**
- "Only 3 Spots Left This Month"
- "This Bonus Disappears in [Time]"

## Awareness Analysis Workflow

Before writing any copy:

1. **Identify the traffic source** — Where are visitors coming from?
2. **Determine awareness level** — Use the matrix above
3. **Assess market sophistication** — How jaded is this audience?
4. **Select headline approach** — Match to awareness
5. **Plan copy length** — More awareness = shorter copy
6. **Structure with AIDA** — Guide through the journey

## Transitioning Between Levels

Copy can move prospects through awareness stages. Each email, ad, or content piece should move them ONE stage forward:

- **Unaware → Problem Aware**: Reveal the hidden problem
- **Problem Aware → Solution Aware**: Show solutions exist
- **Solution Aware → Product Aware**: Differentiate your approach
- **Product Aware → Most Aware**: Remove friction, create urgency

## Awareness Diagnostic Questions

1. **Do they know they have a problem?** No → Unaware
2. **Are they actively looking for solutions?** No → Problem Aware
3. **Do they know YOUR solution exists?** No → Solution Aware
4. **Have they engaged with your content/product?** No → Product Aware, Yes → Most Aware
