# Dan Kennedy Copywriter

Deep expertise in Dan Kennedy's direct response copywriting methodology for creating copy that sells.

## When to Use

- Writing landing page copy
- Creating sales pages or long-form sales letters
- Crafting email sequences
- Developing offers and CTAs
- Writing headlines that grab attention
- Adding urgency without being sleazy

## Core Principles

### Write Backwards
Start from the customer's fears, desires, and frustrations — then reveal the solution tied to the offer. Never start with the product, features, or company.

### Write Emotionally, Not Factually
Use conversational language with enthusiasm. Write like talking to a friend, not a corporate brochure.

### Make Bold, Specific Claims
Be confident, not timid. Specificity makes claims believable — use numbers, timeframes, and results.

### Message-to-Market Match
Each audience believes their situation is unique. Customize copy for different segments.

## The PAS Framework

### Problem
Identify and articulate the prospect's pain point. Be specific about what's wrong in their current situation.

### Agitate
Make them feel the cost of inaction. What happens if they don't solve this? What are they missing? How will it get worse?

### Solution
Present the offer as the answer. Connect it directly to the pain you've agitated.

## Headline Formulas

### Benefit-Driven
```
"How to [Achieve Desired Result] Without [Pain Point]"
→ "How to Generate 50 Leads Per Week Without Cold Calling"

"The [Number] [Timeframe] [System/Method] for [Desired Result]"
→ "The 7-Day System for Doubling Your Email Open Rates"

"[Number] Ways to [Achieve Result] (Even If [Objection])"
→ "5 Ways to Close High-Ticket Clients (Even If You Hate Selling)"
```

### Curiosity
```
"The [Adjective] Secret [Audience] Use to [Achieve Result]"
→ "The Counterintuitive Secret Top Realtors Use to Get Listings"

"Why [Common Belief] Is Dead Wrong (And What to Do Instead)"
→ "Why 'Post Every Day' Is Dead Wrong (And What to Do Instead)"
```

### Problem-Focused
```
"Are You Making These [Number] [Topic] Mistakes?"
→ "Are You Making These 3 Deadly Landing Page Mistakes?"

"[Pain Point]? Here's the Fix That Works in [Timeframe]"
→ "No Leads? Here's the Fix That Works in 14 Days"
```

### Specificity
```
"How [Specific Person] [Achieved Specific Result] in [Specific Timeframe]"
→ "How a Struggling Coach Generated $127,000 in 90 Days"

"Get [Specific Result] or [Guarantee]"
→ "Get 25 Qualified Appointments in 30 Days or Your Money Back"
```

## Offer Structure

Every offer must include:

1. **The Promise**: What transformation or result do they get?
2. **The Mechanism**: Why does this work? (The unique approach)
3. **Proof**: Why should they believe you? (Testimonials, results, credentials)
4. **Urgency**: Why act now? (Deadline, limited spots, bonus expiring)
5. **Risk Reversal**: What guarantee removes their fear?

### The Irresistible Offer Formula

**Value Stack:**
- Main product/service (Value: $X)
- Bonus #1: Complementary item (Value: $X)
- Bonus #2: Accelerator item (Value: $X)
- Bonus #3: Support item (Value: $X)
- Total Value: $X,XXX

**The Ask:**
"You won't pay $X,XXX. You won't even pay $XXX. Today, you get everything for just $XX."

**Risk Reversal:**
"If you don't [achieve result] within [timeframe], full refund. No questions asked."

### The "Reason Why" Offer
Always give a reason for the deal — a reason (even a weak one) increases response dramatically:
- "Because I'm launching this program..."
- "Because I want 50 case studies..."
- "Because my calendar has 3 openings this month..."

## CTA Writing Rules

- Use action verbs: "Get", "Claim", "Start", "Download", "Grab"
- Answer "What happens when I click?"
- Reduce friction with reassurance
- Repeat CTAs throughout long-form copy
- Button text should complete: "I want to ___"

### High-Converting Button Text
- "Get Instant Access"
- "Claim Your Spot"
- "Start My Free Trial"
- "Send Me the Blueprint"
- "Reserve My Seat"
- "Unlock the Training"

### Button + Reassurance Combos
- Button: "Get Instant Access" / Below: "100% free. No credit card required."
- Button: "Start My 14-Day Trial" / Below: "Cancel anytime. No questions asked."
- Button: "Claim Your Spot" / Below: "Only 12 seats remaining"

## Urgency Tactics (Ethical Only)

**Valid urgency:**
- Real deadlines (webinar starts, doors close)
- Limited inventory (coaching spots, physical products)
- Expiring bonuses (early bird pricing)
- Seasonal relevance (tax deadline, new year)

**Layer multiple elements (Deadline Stack):**
1. Hard deadline: "Doors close Friday at midnight"
2. Bonus deadline: "First 20 get the advanced module free"
3. Price deadline: "Price goes up by $200 on Monday"
4. Calendar deadline: "We start the cohort on the 15th"

**Never fake:**
- Countdown timers that reset
- "Only 3 left" when unlimited
- Fake scarcity on digital products

## Copy Patterns

### The "If/Then" Pattern
"If you're [situation], then you already know [problem]. And you've probably tried [failed solutions]. But what if there was a way to [desired result] without [pain point]?"

### The "Imagine" Pattern
"Imagine waking up and checking your phone to see [positive scenario]. Imagine [another positive outcome]. Now imagine [the ultimate result]. This isn't a fantasy. It's exactly what happens when you [use the solution]."

### The "Problem Amplification" Pattern
"Every day you don't solve [problem], you're losing [specific cost]. That's [monthly cost] per month. [Yearly cost] per year. And it only gets worse because [compounding factor]."

### The "Bridge" Pattern
- **Before:** Current painful state with vivid details
- **After:** Desired state with specific outcomes
- **Bridge:** Your solution that gets them from before to after

## Copy Length Guidelines

| Price Point | Copy Length | Format |
|-------------|------------|--------|
| Free | Short (300–500 words) | Bullet points, quick benefits |
| $1–$50 | Medium (500–1000 words) | Benefits + light proof |
| $50–$500 | Long (1000–3000 words) | Full PAS + testimonials |
| $500–$2000 | Very Long (3000–5000 words) | Complete sales letter |
| $2000+ | Ultra Long (5000+ words) | Multi-page sales letter + video |

The more money asked, the more convincing required. Every sentence must earn its place.

## Section Checklist

Every section of copy should answer:
- "So what?" (Why does this matter to me?)
- "Why should I care?" (What's in it for me?)
- "Prove it" (Where's the evidence?)

## Power Words

- **Urgency:** Now, Today, Immediately, Limited, Deadline, Last chance, Before it's gone
- **Value:** Free, Bonus, Exclusive, Premium, Proven, Guaranteed, Secret, Insider
- **Emotion:** Discover, Reveal, Unlock, Transform, Breakthrough, Finally, Imagine, Escape
- **Trust:** Proven, Tested, Guaranteed, Risk-free, Certified, Verified, Endorsed
